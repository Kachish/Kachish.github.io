T-flex App: Revolutionizing Delivery Services

The T-flex App is a mobile application designed to provide seamless and secure delivery services.
Launched between August 1st and 19th, 2023, it connects senders and delivery personnel in Nairobi, featuring user verification, a delivery marketplace, real-time tracking, and interactive communication tools.
Designed with Figma, the app offers flexible earning opportunities for delivery personnel and ensures efficient service for both individuals and businesses.

Project Overview

My mission with the T-flex Delivery Service is to connect senders and delivery personnel through a user-friendly platform, facilitating orderly and secure package delivery across Nairobi.
This service targets both individual and business senders, providing flexible earning opportunities for delivery personnel.
The MVP includes key features such as user verification, a delivery marketplace, interactive communication tools, real-time tracking, and a rewards system to incentivize user engagement. Scalability ensures potential for future feature expansion.

The Problem and The Goal

The T-flex project addresses the critical challenges faced by the delivery service industry in Africa, including limited access to technology, high operational costs, and fierce competition.
These obstacles make it difficult for companies to offer reliable and affordable services. Recognizing the growing market and the high demand for delivery solutions, T-flex aims to provide a comprehensive, flexible, and cost-effective delivery service.
The project's goal is to deliver an all-rounded solution that leverages advanced technology to ensure efficiency, affordability, and high user satisfaction, setting a new standard in the African delivery service landscape.

Design Process

Design journey for the T-flex App followed a comprehensive approach:

  • Empathize: Conducted user research, competitive analysis, and identified pain points.
  • Define: Defined the Minimum Viable Product (MVP), user flow, and created a sitemap.
  • Ideate: Created sketches and low-fidelity prototypes.
  • Prototype: Developed low and high-fidelity prototypes and conducted usability testing.
  • Evaluate: Performed heuristic evaluations and provided recommendations for improvements.

User Research Pillars

User research for the T-flex project is structured around four essential pillars to ensure the delivery service aligns with business objectives and user needs.
The first pillar, Business Goals, focuses on positioning FLEX as a competitive and innovative solution in the African market. The second pillar, Project Scope, defines the boundaries and extent of T-flex functionalities, MVP features, and user interactions.
The third pillar, User Persona, involves gaining insights into user behaviors, motivations, preferences, and challenges.
The fourth pillar, Pain Point Validation, ensures the product addresses user needs, enhances satisfaction, and boosts engagement. This rigorous research approach ensures a transformative delivery service experience.

Pain Points

The T-flex project identifies and addresses key pain points to enhance the delivery service experience.
Pain Point 1 highlights the cumbersome and time-consuming process for senders, leading to delays.
Pain Point 2 addresses the limited job opportunities for delivery personnel, resulting in inconsistent income.
Pain Point 3 points out the limited access to technology, making it difficult for both senders and delivery personnel to track deliveries and place orders conveniently.
Pain Point 4 focuses on the lack of real-time tracking capabilities, which challenges users in monitoring and choosing suitable delivery options.
By tackling these issues, T-flex aims to streamline operations and improve user satisfaction.

User Persona - Sarah Bukhari

The user persona of Sarah Bukhari represents a busy family physician in Nairobi, Kenya, who frequently uses online platforms for quick and efficient task completion.
Sarah's goals include sending thoughtful gifts on time, using a convenient platform to connect with delivery riders, and having transparency in the delivery process.
Her frustrations involve delays, rider cancellations, and lack of transparency.
Motivated by ease, speed, and transparency, Sarah prefers using her phone and computer for delivery services.
This persona helps T-flex tailor its services to meet the specific needs and preferences of users like Sarah, ensuring a user-centric approach to product development.

User Persona: David Almasi

This user persona provides a detailed profile of David Almasi, a 32-year-old delivery cyclist from Juja, Kenya. As a reliable and trustworthy delivery service provider, David is dedicated to meeting his clients' needs while navigating the streets with ease.
His goals include expanding his network, improving communication with clients, and exploring additional income opportunities.
However, David faces challenges such as inconsistent job availability, physical strain from deliveries, and communication issues that lead to delays.
His motivations are centered around ease, speed, and flexibility, preferring phone-based interactions.
This persona highlights David's professional aspirations and the obstacles he encounters, offering insight into the needs of similar delivery service providers.

Competitive Audit: Delivery Services Analysis

This competitive audit evaluates the delivery service offerings of four companies: Pickup Mtaani, Ngamia Haulers, DHL, and Bolt.
It assesses key features such as delivery tracking, rating and reviews, scheduling options, user interface, and alternative recipient contact methods.
The audit identifies gaps in service offerings and highlights opportunities for improvement, such as providing a more intuitive app interface and accurate delivery tracking.
By addressing these areas, businesses can enhance their service quality, increase customer satisfaction, and differentiate themselves in a competitive market.
This analysis serves as a strategic tool for optimizing delivery service offerings and improving overall user experience.

Lo-Fi & Mi-Fi Usability Test Summary

Insight-Driven Enhancements** Through rigorous usability testing of low-fidelity and mid-fidelity prototypes, invaluable insights were gathered to refine the T-Flex app.
The usability tests aimed to evaluate how easily users could complete specified tasks, the clarity and effectiveness of task instructions, and gather participants' feedback and suggestions.
Key improvements included streamlining the sender/delivery screens by consolidating all relevant details onto a single screen to enhance user experience and reduce loading times.
The sender's side credit card section was redesigned to include a more user-friendly interface for inputting credit card details.
Additionally, implementing an M-Pesa SDK push for payments and incorporating a wallet and dashboard for the delivery side were crucial enhancements.
These changes, guided by user feedback, ensure the app meets the highest standards of functionality and user satisfaction.
